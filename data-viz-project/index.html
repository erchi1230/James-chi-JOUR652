Walk puppies with purpose

UMD students foster future guide dogs

By James Chi

A group of people with small puppies just appeared at the IKEA College Park and started to strolling around inside the shopping area. Puppies walked pass aisles and stairs, following their owners to all kinds of places. One lady even waved a stuffed dog to in front of them, trying to distract their attention.

It turns out that they are guide dog puppies that are trained and fostered by the organization Terps Raising Pups, the student chapter of the Guide Dog Foundation for the Blind (GDF) at the University of Maryland. The foundation, founded for more than 70 years, has been dedicated to breed, raise, train, and place assistance dogs without charging any fee. As the GDF’s second-biggest college program, Terps Raising Pups handles more than 20 pups on campus and has been expanding rapidly as more students joining as puppy raisers.

Get puppies ready for their next stage

The purpose of the college puppy raising program is to equip puppies with the qualities of a good guide dog. “Basic obedience, exposure to different environments, and (having) great house manners, that’s the three big things we go for.”. Melissa Smith, the area coordinator of the organization, explained their major goals. These three things are seen as an important foundation to become a qualified guide dog.
